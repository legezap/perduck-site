The Challenge

A Pavilion for an Ecosystem, Not Just a Brand

Microsoft Arabia needed a self-contained partner-centric pavilion at LEAP Riyadh — not a standard exhibition stand, but a fully operational venue within a venue. The brief required dedicated, individually branded zones for eight Microsoft partners and their Arabia-region teams, each showcasing independent technology solutions while maintaining a cohesive Microsoft visual identity legible from every approach angle on the 35,000+ sqm show floor. The pavilion also needed to function as a networking hub for Microsoft Arabia employees and partner representatives, with open catering areas and multiple private meeting rooms.

The structural scope was significant: a 700 sqm double-decker build at Riyadh Front Exhibition Center required full engineering calculations submitted to venue management, including steel I-beam framework design rated for an upper-deck floor loading of 350 kg/sqm minimum to support meeting rooms, hospitality furniture, and peak-traffic foot loads. Saudi building code compliance demanded independent structural certification separate from the standard DWTC or ADNEC approval processes our team was accustomed to, with additional fire-safety, emergency egress, and load-distribution documentation reviewed by Riyadh Front's technical authority.

The AV integration alone was a project within a project: eight partner zones each requiring independent LED video wall installations, a centralized media server rack, and a total power distribution system drawing over 400A across three-phase supply from the venue's main distribution board. The timeline compressed everything — three weeks of design development, three weeks of parallel fabrication across steel, graphics, and AV, and a five-day on-site installation window with a crew of 35 working in coordinated shifts to meet an immovable show-opening deadline.

Our Solution

Engineering a Partner-Centric Technology Pavilion

Our approach began with a zoning masterplan developed in collaboration with Microsoft Arabia's event team. We mapped the visitor journey through eight distinct partner zones on the ground floor, each allocated between 40 and 80 sqm depending on partner tier, plus a central atrium entrance with a 6-meter-high branded header visible from all four approaching aisles. Each partner zone was designed with independent electrical circuits (32A single-phase per zone), dedicated LED video walls, and zone-specific lighting — so a single technical fault in one area could never cascade across the pavilion.

The primary structure used 200x200mm steel I-beam columns on 6-meter grid spacing, with IPE 240 steel beams supporting the upper-deck floor plate. Structural calculations were performed to achieve a certified floor loading of 350 kg/sqm on the upper deck — 40% above the Riyadh Front venue's baseline requirement — providing safety margin for the hospitality lounge furniture, the five private meeting rooms, and simultaneous foot traffic during peak attendance windows. The upper deck floor was constructed from 38mm marine-grade plywood panels on steel joists, overlaid with carpet tiles and a 10mm acoustic underlay in the meeting rooms. Two architectural staircases at opposite ends of the pavilion featured open-tread steel construction with LED-lit risers and tempered glass balustrades with brushed stainless-steel handrails, each rated for bi-directional traffic flow of 60 persons per minute.

Graphics production ran in parallel with structural fabrication across three facilities. We produced 320 sqm of large-format printed graphics — 180 sqm of dye-sublimation tension-fabric for the backlit lightbox displays spanning the pavilion's full rear wall height, and 140 sqm of UV-flatbed printed rigid panels for partner zone branding and wayfinding. Six LED video wall installations used 2.6mm pixel-pitch panels: three ground-floor walls (ranging from 3x2m to 5x3m) for partner product demonstrations, and three upper-level walls for presentations and ambient Microsoft branding. All six walls were fed from a centralized Barco media server rack positioned in a concealed technical room beneath the main staircase, giving Microsoft's AV team real-time content switching across any screen combination from a single control interface.

The overhead lighting rig used 12-inch aluminum box truss spanning the pavilion perimeter at 4.5 meters above finished floor level, supporting a dual-layer lighting design: 48 adjustable 30W LED spot fixtures (4000K, 90+ CRI) for the partner demo areas delivering 800+ lux on product surfaces, and 36 RGBW wash fixtures for the hospitality zones programmable to shift between 3000K warm ambient and Microsoft-branded accent colors during keynote sessions. Power distribution ran from a dedicated 400A three-phase supply through four sub-distribution boards — one per quadrant of the pavilion — each with independent RCD protection and manual isolation switches accessible to our on-site electrical team.

The upper-deck hospitality lounge seated 40 for open networking, with a fully plumbed coffee bar featuring branded countertop fabricated from white Corian solid-surface material. Five enclosed meeting rooms (three seating six, two seating ten) used 100mm fabric-wrapped acoustic partition walls achieving an STC rating of 38, sufficient to enable confidential business discussions during peak show-floor noise. Custom joinery throughout the meeting rooms included integrated cable management, flush-mounted power and USB outlets in each table surface, and wall-mounted 55-inch displays with HDMI and wireless casting capability. Flooring throughout the upper deck used 6mm commercial carpet tiles in Microsoft's brand-neutral grey, with 10mm rubber acoustic underlay reducing impact noise transmission to the ground floor below.

International logistics coordinated shipments from three fabrication facilities: 14 tons of structural steel from our primary workshop in Al Quoz, 320 sqm of printed graphics from our production partner, and 2.4 tons of AV equipment sourced from manufacturer warehouses in Europe — all converging on Riyadh Front within a 48-hour delivery window. On-site installation ran five consecutive days with a 35-person crew working 14-hour shifts: Day 1 for steel erection and floor plate installation, Day 2 for upper-deck completion and staircase assembly, Day 3 for cladding and graphics, Day 4 for electrical fit-out and AV mounting, and Day 5 for lighting focus, AV commissioning, and furniture placement. Each partner zone was individually handed over to its respective brand team for final merchandising and content loading 24 hours before doors opened.

Results

Delivered on Time. Exceeded Expectations.

The Microsoft pavilion at LEAP was delivered on schedule and fully operational 24 hours before show opening — a critical requirement given the complexity of the multi-partner handover process. Each of the partner zones was individually commissioned and approved by the respective brand teams without requiring any post-installation modifications.

Visitor throughput during the three-day event exceeded Microsoft's projections, with the ground-floor layout handling peak crowds without bottlenecking at any point in the visitor journey. The hospitality lounge hosted over 120 executive-level meetings across the event, with attendees noting the acoustic quality and privacy of the upper-deck environment.

The pavilion was the largest single-brand build at LEAP that year, and its architectural staircase became a recognizable landmark on the show floor — a natural wayfinding reference point for attendees navigating the exhibition halls. Multiple industry publications featured the pavilion in their event coverage.

This project reinforced our capability for large-scale, multi-stakeholder exhibition builds in international venues and established a working relationship with Microsoft's regional events team for future activations across the GCC.
