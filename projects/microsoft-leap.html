Microsoft @ LEAP Saudi Arabia

A 700 sqm double-decker partner pavilion in Riyadh — our largest build to date and a landmark deployment for one of the world's most recognized technology brands.

The Challenge

A Pavilion for an Ecosystem, Not Just a Brand

Microsoft needed more than an exhibition stand at LEAP — they needed a self-contained pavilion that could house their entire partner ecosystem under one roof. The brief demanded dedicated zones for each Microsoft partner to showcase their solutions independently, while maintaining a cohesive visual identity that unmistakably read as Microsoft from any approach angle on the show floor.

The venue presented its own constraints: a 700 sqm plot in Riyadh's exhibition center required full structural engineering sign-off for double-decker construction, compliance with Saudi building regulations, and coordination with venue rigging and power infrastructure that differed from the Dubai and Abu Dhabi venues our team was most familiar with.

Beyond the physical build, the client required seamless AV integration across multiple demo stations, a premium hospitality lounge for VIP hosting, and a ground-floor layout optimized for high-volume visitor throughput during peak hours. The timeline was aggressive — from signed brief to show-floor-ready in under eight weeks, including international logistics and on-site installation.

Our Solution

Engineering a Partner-Centric Technology Pavilion

Our approach began with the floor plan. Working closely with Microsoft's event team, we mapped the visitor journey through the pavilion — from the entrance atrium through each partner zone, past the live demo stations, and up to the second-floor hospitality and meeting areas. Every square meter was assigned a function before a single structural beam was specified.

The double-decker structure was engineered using steel I-beam framing with a certified load capacity that exceeded venue requirements by 40%, giving us headroom for the heavy AV equipment mounted on the upper deck. The architectural staircase connecting the two levels was designed as a visual statement: open-tread, LED-lit risers that drew eyes upward and created a natural flow of traffic to the upper-level meeting rooms.

Graphics production ran in parallel with structural fabrication. We produced over 280 sqm of large-format printed graphics, including tension-fabric lightbox displays that spanned the full height of the pavilion's rear wall. LED video walls were installed across six locations — three on the ground floor for partner demos and three on the upper level for presentations and ambient branding. The entire AV system was controlled from a centralized hub, allowing Microsoft's team to switch content in real-time across any screen in the pavilion.

Trussing for the overhead lighting rig was designed to support both theatrical spot lighting for the demo areas and ambient wash lighting for the hospitality zones. Power distribution required a dedicated 400A three-phase supply from the venue, with independent circuits for each partner zone to prevent any single point of failure from taking down the entire pavilion.

The hospitality lounge on the upper deck featured custom joinery, branded furniture, a fully equipped coffee bar, and acoustic treatment to create a private meeting environment despite being on an open show floor. We specified carpet tiles with a 10mm acoustic underlay for the meeting rooms and used fabric-wrapped acoustic panels on the partition walls to reduce ambient noise bleed.

International logistics for this project involved coordinating shipments from three fabrication facilities — structural steel from our primary workshop, graphics from our print partner, and AV equipment sourced directly from manufacturer warehouses — all converging on the Riyadh venue within a 48-hour delivery window. On-site installation ran over five days with a crew of 35, with each partner zone handed over to the respective brand team for final merchandising and content loading 24 hours before doors opened.

Results

Delivered on Time. Exceeded Expectations.

The Microsoft pavilion at LEAP was delivered on schedule and fully operational 24 hours before show opening — a critical requirement given the complexity of the multi-partner handover process. Each of the partner zones was individually commissioned and approved by the respective brand teams without requiring any post-installation modifications.

Visitor throughput during the three-day event exceeded Microsoft's projections, with the ground-floor layout handling peak crowds without bottlenecking at any point in the visitor journey. The hospitality lounge hosted over 120 executive-level meetings across the event, with attendees noting the acoustic quality and privacy of the upper-deck environment.

The pavilion was the largest single-brand build at LEAP that year, and its architectural staircase became a recognizable landmark on the show floor — a natural wayfinding reference point for attendees navigating the exhibition halls. Multiple industry publications featured the pavilion in their event coverage.

This project reinforced our capability for large-scale, multi-stakeholder exhibition builds in international venues and established a working relationship with Microsoft's regional events team for future activations across the GCC.
