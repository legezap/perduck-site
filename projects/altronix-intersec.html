Altronix @ Intersec Dubai

A 25 sqm stand that proved compact does not mean compromise — dramatic LED integration turned a modest footprint into a commanding presence on the security exhibition floor.

The Challenge

Making 25 Square Meters Impossible to Ignore

Intersec Dubai is the world's leading trade fair for security, safety, and fire protection — a massive event where exhibitors range from multinational defense contractors with 200+ sqm island stands to niche technology providers in standard shell schemes. Altronix, a recognized name in power supply and access control solutions, had a 25 sqm plot at stand S2-D18 — a challenging position in a crowded hall where larger competitors dominated the sightlines.

The brief was deceptively simple: make Altronix look like a major player despite having one of the smaller custom stands on the floor. The products — power supplies, access control boards, and surveillance system components — are technically sophisticated but visually compact. There was no large-scale hardware to create a natural visual anchor. Everything had to come from the stand design itself.

Beyond visual impact, Altronix needed the stand to function as a working product demonstration environment. System integrators and distributors visiting Intersec expected to see live product demos, not just display units behind glass. The stand also needed a semi-private meeting area where sales representatives could discuss technical specifications and pricing with qualified buyers.

Our Solution

LED-Driven Design for Maximum Visibility

Our approach was built around a single principle: if the products are small, the light has to be big. We designed the entire stand around a dramatic blue LED lighting scheme that transformed the 25 sqm space into a glowing beacon on the Intersec floor. The blue was not arbitrary — it aligned with Altronix's established brand palette and, more importantly, it created a strong visual contrast against the predominantly neutral and grey tones of the security exhibition environment.

The rear wall was constructed as a full-height LED-backlit panel system using edge-lit acrylic panels with blue LED strip lighting concealed behind diffusion layers. This created an even, glowing surface that served as both the brand backdrop and the product display system. Individual product units were mounted on transparent acrylic shelves that appeared to float against the illuminated wall, giving each product its own spotlight moment while maintaining a clean, unified visual plane.

Floor plan optimization was critical at this scale. We divided the 25 sqm into three functional zones using visual cues rather than physical partitions: a product display wall along the rear, a central engagement zone with a branded counter for live demonstrations, and a corner meeting area with a small table and seating for four. Sight lines from the aisle were carefully studied — every product display was visible from the primary approach angle, and the LED glow was calibrated to be visible from at least 20 meters down the hall aisle.

The overhead structure used a minimal trussing rig to maintain ceiling height and avoid the closed-in feeling that plagues many compact stands. We mounted additional LED downlights on the truss to provide task lighting for the demo counter and meeting area, while the perimeter LED panels handled ambient illumination. The total electrical load was surprisingly modest — the entire LED system drew under 2kW, well within the venue's standard power allocation for a 25 sqm plot.

Graphics production was intentionally restrained. Rather than covering every surface in printed messaging, we used the LED-lit surfaces themselves as the design feature and limited printed graphics to the header fascia and a single backlit logo panel. Product information was displayed on a monitor integrated into the demo counter, where sales staff could switch between product data sheets, installation videos, and company presentations depending on the visitor's interest.

Material selection emphasized quality over quantity. The counter was fabricated from white solid-surface material with a blue LED accent strip along its base, reinforcing the color scheme. Flooring was a dark charcoal raised-access floor that absorbed light and made the blue LED elements appear even more vivid by contrast. Every material decision was made in service of the lighting concept — nothing competed with the glow.

Results

Small Stand, Big Impression

The Altronix stand at Intersec delivered on its core objective: it looked and felt like a premium presence despite occupying one of the smaller custom plots on the floor. The blue LED scheme created an unmistakable visual signature that drew visitors from the main aisles — multiple attendees reported that the stand was their first stop when entering the security technology hall specifically because the lighting caught their attention from across the space.

Product engagement was strong. The live demo counter facilitated over 50 qualified product demonstrations per day, with the integrated monitor allowing sales staff to quickly pivot between product categories based on each visitor's specific requirements. The floating acrylic shelves proved effective at showcasing the compact hardware — visitors frequently commented on the display quality and the way the lighting made each product feel like a premium consumer electronics product rather than industrial infrastructure.

The meeting area, despite being only a corner of a 25 sqm stand, was booked solid throughout the event. The dark flooring and the recessed position relative to the bright LED display wall created a naturally quieter, more private zone that functioned surprisingly well for focused business discussions.

This project became a reference case for our compact stand capabilities. It demonstrated that with disciplined floor plan optimization, a commitment to a single strong design concept, and precise execution of lighting and materials, a 25 sqm stand can compete for attention against exhibitors with five or ten times the space. Altronix's regional marketing team subsequently adopted the LED-centric design language for their stands at other regional exhibitions.
