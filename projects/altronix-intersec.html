The Challenge

Making 25 Square Meters Impossible to Ignore

Intersec Dubai 2025 (January 14-16 at DWTC) is the world's leading trade fair for security, safety, and fire protection — a massive event where exhibitors range from multinational defense contractors commanding 200+ sqm island pavilions to niche technology providers in standard 9 sqm shell schemes. Altronix Corp, a recognized leader in power and data transmission solutions for the professional security industry, had a 25 sqm plot at stand S2-D18 in Hall 2 — a mid-aisle position surrounded by larger competitors whose stands blocked direct sightlines from the hall's primary entrance and cross-aisle intersections.

The product challenge was the defining constraint. Altronix's portfolio — power supplies for access control systems, PoE midspans, fire alarm NAC power extenders, and networked power controllers — is technically sophisticated but physically compact. The largest unit in their display range measured roughly 400x300x100mm. There was no towering hardware, no vehicle-scale product, no natural visual anchor. With 25 sqm and products that could fit in a shoebox, every gram of visual impact had to come from the stand design, material selection, and lighting engineering.

Functionally, Altronix needed the stand to operate as a working demonstration environment for the three-day event. System integrators and security distributors visiting Intersec expected to see live product demos — powered units running with connected loads, not just unplugged display units behind glass. The stand also needed a semi-private meeting zone where sales representatives could discuss technical specifications, system design, and distributor pricing with qualified buyers without being overheard by competitors in adjacent stands.

Our Solution

LED-Driven Design for Maximum Visibility

Our approach was built around a single principle: if the products are small, the light has to be big. We designed the entire stand around a dramatic blue LED lighting scheme that transformed the 25 sqm space into a glowing beacon on the Intersec floor. The blue (approximately Pantone 2935 C, matching Altronix's corporate identity) was not arbitrary — it created a strong chromatic contrast against the predominantly neutral grey and white tones of the security exhibition environment, where most competitors defaulted to safe, monochromatic palettes. In a hall full of grey metal, blue light is a weapon.

The rear wall was constructed as a full-height (3.2m) LED-backlit panel system. The structure used 10mm opal acrylic panels (Plexiglas Satinice 0D010 DF) mounted on an aluminum subframe with 60mm standoff, creating a cavity behind each panel for edge-lit blue LED strip lighting (14.4W/m, 460nm wavelength, mounted top and bottom). A secondary diffusion layer of 3mm milky polycarbonate behind the acrylic ensured even light distribution with zero hot-spotting across the 8.4 sqm illuminated surface area. Individual product units were mounted on 8mm clear acrylic shelves with polished edges, secured to the backlit wall by concealed stainless-steel pin brackets — creating a floating display effect where each power supply or access control board appeared to hover against the glowing blue surface, drawing the eye naturally to the product detail.

Floor plan optimization was critical at this scale. We divided the 25 sqm (5.0m x 5.0m) into three functional zones using visual cues rather than physical partitions: an 8.4 sqm product display wall spanning the full rear elevation, a 10 sqm central engagement zone with a branded demo counter (1800x600mm white Corian surface with recessed 16A power outlets for live product demonstrations), and a 6.6 sqm corner meeting zone with a 900x900mm table and seating for four on upholstered stools. Sight lines from the S2 aisle were studied using 3D walkthrough renders — every product display position was verified visible from the primary approach angle at a 30-degree field of view, and the LED glow intensity was calibrated to be perceptible from 20+ meters down the hall aisle against the 350-lux ambient hall lighting.

The overhead structure used a minimal 290mm aluminum box truss perimeter rig at 3.5 meters above finished floor level — well below DWTC's maximum single-level build height — to maintain an open ceiling and avoid the closed-in feeling that plagues compact stands with full fascia enclosures. We mounted four adjustable 20W LED track spots (4000K, 90+ CRI) on the truss to provide task lighting for the demo counter (delivering 600+ lux on the working surface) and two warm-white downlights (3000K) over the meeting zone. The perimeter backlit panels handled all ambient illumination. The total electrical load for the complete stand — LED wall system, truss lighting, demo counter power, and meeting zone — drew 1.8kW, well within DWTC's standard 3kW power allocation for a 25 sqm plot, meaning Altronix paid for standard power only with no upgrade surcharge.

Graphics production was intentionally restrained — just 6.2 sqm total. Rather than covering every surface in printed messaging, we let the LED-lit surfaces function as the primary design feature. Printed graphics were limited to a 4.0x0.6m header fascia (UV-flatbed on 3mm Dibond) and a single 1.2x1.0m backlit logo panel (dye-sub on tension fabric). All product information was displayed on a 32-inch commercial monitor integrated into the demo counter on a VESA mount, where sales staff could switch between product data sheets, wiring diagrams, installation videos, and company presentations depending on the visitor's system integration requirements.

Material selection emphasized quality over quantity — every surface was specified in service of the lighting concept. The demo counter was fabricated from 12mm white Corian solid-surface material on an MDF substructure, with a blue LED accent strip (matching the wall system wavelength) recessed into a 10mm channel along the counter's base, creating a floating glow effect at floor level. Flooring was dark charcoal vinyl plank (LVT, Karndean Opus series) on a 50mm raised-access floor system that concealed all electrical cabling and provided the essential dark ground-plane that made the blue LED elements appear dramatically more vivid by contrast. Wall surfaces flanking the backlit display used 18mm MDF with matte dark grey HPL laminate (Egger U961 ST2) — zero reflectance, zero competition with the blue glow. The full stand was installed in 14 hours by a crew of six.

Results

Small Stand, Big Impression

The Altronix stand at Intersec delivered on its core objective: it looked and felt like a premium presence despite occupying one of the smaller custom plots on the floor. The blue LED scheme created an unmistakable visual signature that drew visitors from the main aisles — multiple attendees reported that the stand was their first stop when entering the security technology hall specifically because the lighting caught their attention from across the space.

Product engagement was strong. The live demo counter facilitated over 50 qualified product demonstrations per day, with the integrated monitor allowing sales staff to quickly pivot between product categories based on each visitor's specific requirements. The floating acrylic shelves proved effective at showcasing the compact hardware — visitors frequently commented on the display quality and the way the lighting made each product feel like a premium consumer electronics product rather than industrial infrastructure.

The meeting area, despite being only a corner of a 25 sqm stand, was booked solid throughout the event. The dark flooring and the recessed position relative to the bright LED display wall created a naturally quieter, more private zone that functioned surprisingly well for focused business discussions.

This project became a reference case for our compact stand capabilities. It demonstrated that with disciplined floor plan optimization, a commitment to a single strong design concept, and precise execution of lighting and materials, a 25 sqm stand can compete for attention against exhibitors with five or ten times the space. Altronix's regional marketing team subsequently adopted the LED-centric design language for their stands at other regional exhibitions.
