Vanderlande @ Airport Show Dubai

A 60 sqm double-decker stand where structural engineering meets product demonstration — featuring an architectural staircase that became the visual anchor of the Airport Show floor.

The Challenge

Demonstrating Industrial Systems on a Show Floor

Vanderlande is a global market leader in baggage handling systems, warehouse automation, and parcel sorting — the kind of heavy industrial equipment that does not typically translate well to an exhibition environment. Their challenge was clear: demonstrate the precision and reliability of their airport logistics solutions in a 60 sqm footprint at the Airport Show Dubai, while simultaneously providing a professional meeting environment for executive-level discussions with airline and airport authority buyers.

The double-decker requirement was non-negotiable. Vanderlande needed the ground floor entirely dedicated to product demonstrations, which meant all meeting functions had to move to a second level. This created a significant structural engineering challenge: the upper deck needed to support the weight of a furnished meeting room while the ground floor accommodated heavy demo equipment with specific power and data requirements.

Additionally, the staircase connecting the two levels needed to serve double duty — functional access to the upper deck and a visual design element that elevated the stand above the row of standard single-level competitors lining the same aisle.

Our Solution

Precision Engineering for Precision Equipment

The design started with the demo equipment. We obtained full technical specifications for Vanderlande's baggage handling system demo unit — weight, dimensions, power requirements, vibration tolerance, and data connectivity needs — and designed the floor plan around it. The demo area was positioned for maximum visibility from the aisle, with a recessed floor channel for cable management and a dedicated 63A three-phase power supply isolated from the rest of the stand's electrical system.

The structural framework used steel rectangular hollow section (RHS) columns with welded base plates, bolted to a raised floor system that concealed all electrical and data cabling. Floor loading calculations accounted for the combined weight of the demo equipment, the upper-deck meeting room furniture, and a safety margin for foot traffic during peak attendance periods. All structural drawings were submitted to DWTC venue management for approval 30 days before build-in, with zero revision requests — a testament to our engineering team's familiarity with venue requirements.

The architectural staircase was the signature element. Rather than a standard enclosed staircase that would consume valuable ground-floor space, we designed an open-tread steel-and-glass staircase with integrated LED strip lighting along each riser. The balustrade used tempered glass panels with brushed stainless-steel handrails, creating a transparent visual line that made the upper deck feel connected to the ground floor rather than separated from it.

Graphics production for this build prioritized technical credibility. Large-format printed graphics featured Vanderlande's system schematics and process diagrams alongside brand messaging, reinforcing the engineering-first identity of the company. Backlit fabric panels along the perimeter walls provided even illumination and a clean, institutional aesthetic appropriate for the aviation industry audience.

The upper-level meeting area was designed for focused business conversations. We specified acoustic ceiling tiles and upholstered partition walls to reduce noise transmission from the show floor below. The meeting table was custom-built to accommodate Vanderlande's presentation equipment, including integrated power outlets, an HDMI connection to a wall-mounted display, and concealed cable management.

Rigging for the overhead lighting was coordinated with the venue's ceiling grid to ensure even illumination across both levels. We used a combination of adjustable LED spotlights for the demo equipment — highlighting mechanical details and moving parts — and warm-tone ambient fixtures for the meeting area to create a comfortable, professional atmosphere. The full installation was completed in three days with a crew of twelve, including structural assembly, electrical fit-out, graphics installation, and AV commissioning.

Results

Engineering Meets Exhibition Impact

The Vanderlande stand was delivered on schedule with all demo equipment commissioned and operational during the pre-show technical check. The baggage handling system demonstration ran continuously throughout the three-day event without a single technical interruption — a direct result of the isolated power and data infrastructure we specified during the design phase.

The architectural staircase achieved exactly what it was designed to do: it drew attention from the main aisle, differentiated Vanderlande from adjacent single-level stands, and created a natural traffic pattern that guided visitors through the demo area before directing them to the upper-level meeting rooms for deeper engagement.

The upper-deck meeting suite hosted a full schedule of appointments throughout the event, with Vanderlande's sales team reporting that the acoustically treated environment was a significant improvement over their previous open-floor meeting areas at the same exhibition. Several meetings with major airport authority buyers were conducted in the privacy of the upper level — conversations that would not have been possible in a standard open-booth configuration.

This project demonstrated our ability to integrate heavy industrial demonstration equipment into a refined exhibition environment without compromising on either the technical requirements of the demo or the design quality of the stand. Vanderlande subsequently engaged us for their next regional exhibition appearance.
