Vanderlande @ Airport Show Dubai

The Challenge

Demonstrating Industrial Systems on a Show Floor

Vanderlande needed to demonstrate their end-to-end passenger and baggage operations vision at Airport Show Dubai — including live demonstrations of their VIA Digital Suite, FLEET Batch autonomous vehicle technology, and BAGLOAD robotic handling systems. These are industrial-scale airport logistics solutions involving automation, digitalization, and AI-driven controls. Translating that into a compelling 60 sqm exhibition experience at DWTC required solving a fundamental problem: how to run working demonstrations of heavy, power-hungry equipment while maintaining a refined enough environment to host executive meetings with airline CEOs and airport authority procurement directors.

The double-decker requirement was non-negotiable. Vanderlande's demo equipment — including a scaled baggage handling conveyor system weighing approximately 380 kg, plus interactive digital kiosks for the VIA Digital Suite — demanded the entire ground floor. All meeting functions had to move to a second level. This created a structural engineering challenge within the DWTC venue framework: the 60 sqm base footprint needed steel columns positioned to avoid interference with the ground-floor demo layout, while the upper deck had to support a furnished 8-person meeting suite with full AV integration. Structural drawings were submitted to DWTC venue management 30 days before build-in and approved without revision — reflecting our engineering team's deep familiarity with DWTC double-decker regulations, including maximum build height of 5.5 meters, minimum staircase width of 1.0 meter, and mandatory secondary emergency egress.

The staircase connecting the two levels needed to serve triple duty — primary access to the upper deck, a visual design statement that elevated Vanderlande above the row of single-level competitors on the same aisle, and a structural element that did not consume more than 4 sqm of the ground-floor demo area. Every square meter on the ground floor was too valuable for the demo equipment layout to sacrifice to a conventional enclosed staircase.

Our Solution

Precision Engineering for Precision Equipment

The design started with Vanderlande's demo equipment specifications. We obtained full technical data for the scaled baggage handling conveyor system (380 kg, 4.2m x 1.8m footprint, 200mm vibration clearance required on all sides), the FLEET Batch autonomous vehicle display unit, and three interactive VIA Digital Suite kiosks each requiring dedicated Cat6 ethernet and 16A single-phase power. The ground-floor plan was engineered around these fixed equipment positions, with the conveyor demo placed at 45 degrees to the primary aisle for maximum visibility. A 150mm raised floor system with recessed cable channels ran the full 60 sqm footprint, concealing 63A three-phase power supply cabling for the demo equipment on an isolated circuit, plus separate 32A circuits for lighting and general power — ensuring the conveyor's motor-driven components could not introduce electrical noise into the digital kiosk displays.

The structural framework used 100x100mm steel rectangular hollow section (RHS) columns with 12mm welded base plates, bolted through the raised floor into the venue's concrete slab at eight anchor points. The upper deck was constructed from 22mm structural plywood on steel C-channel joists at 400mm centers, achieving a rated floor loading of 300 kg/sqm — sufficient for the 8-person meeting suite, a wall-mounted 65-inch display, and the custom presentation table with integrated power and HDMI connectivity. Total structural steel weight for the double-decker frame was approximately 1.8 tons, fabricated and pre-assembled in our Al Quoz workshop before being transported to DWTC in sectional components for rapid on-site erection.

The architectural staircase was the signature element. We designed an open-tread construction using 6mm laser-cut steel plate treads welded to twin steel stringers, with 12mm tempered glass balustrade panels secured by brushed stainless-steel standoff fittings and a 42mm diameter stainless handrail. Each of the 14 treads incorporated a recessed LED strip channel along the riser face, producing a continuous glow effect using 3000K warm-white LED tape at 14.4W per meter. The staircase consumed just 3.6 sqm of ground-floor area — 40% less than a conventional enclosed staircase — while providing a 1.0-meter-wide clear walking width that met DWTC's minimum requirement for public-access double-decker stands.

Graphics production totaled 48 sqm of printed material, prioritizing technical credibility over marketing imagery. We produced 28 sqm of dye-sublimation tension-fabric panels for perimeter backlit lightboxes — featuring Vanderlande's system schematics, airport process flow diagrams, and operational data visualizations printed at 720 DPI on Endutex blockout fabric. An additional 20 sqm of UV-flatbed printed aluminum composite panels (3mm Dibond) were used for structural column wraps and header fascia, delivering crisp Vanderlande branding at a viewing distance of 15+ meters down the DWTC hall aisle. The backlit fabric panels used 6500K daylight-balanced LED modules behind the diffusion layer, producing an institutional, technical aesthetic consistent with the aviation industry audience.

The upper-level meeting suite was designed for focused business conversations with airline and airport authority procurement teams. The 3.8m x 4.2m enclosed room featured 50mm acoustic ceiling tiles (NRC 0.85) and 75mm fabric-wrapped wall panels on two sides, reducing ambient show-floor noise transmission by approximately 18 dB. The custom meeting table seated eight and was fabricated from 25mm MDF with white HPL laminate finish, with two flush-mounted power/data modules providing 230V outlets, USB-A charging, and HDMI pass-through to the wall-mounted 65-inch Samsung commercial display. Cable management ran through the table's hollow steel legs into the raised floor void, keeping the room completely clean of visible wiring.

Overhead lighting was coordinated with DWTC's ceiling rigging grid at the permitted attachment points. We specified 12 adjustable 25W LED track spots (5000K, 90+ CRI) for the ground-floor demo area — focused on the conveyor system's mechanical components and moving parts to highlight precision engineering — and 6 recessed LED downlights (3000K, dimmable) in the upper meeting room ceiling for a warm, professional atmosphere. The full installation was completed in three days with a crew of twelve: Day 1 for structural steel erection and raised floor, Day 2 for cladding, graphics, and electrical first-fix, Day 3 for AV installation, lighting focus, and final commissioning of all demo equipment.

Results

Engineering Meets Exhibition Impact

The Vanderlande stand was delivered on schedule with all demo equipment commissioned and operational during the pre-show technical check. The baggage handling system demonstration ran continuously throughout the three-day event without a single technical interruption — a direct result of the isolated power and data infrastructure we specified during the design phase.

The architectural staircase achieved exactly what it was designed to do: it drew attention from the main aisle, differentiated Vanderlande from adjacent single-level stands, and created a natural traffic pattern that guided visitors through the demo area before directing them to the upper-level meeting rooms for deeper engagement.

The upper-deck meeting suite hosted a full schedule of appointments throughout the event, with Vanderlande's sales team reporting that the acoustically treated environment was a significant improvement over their previous open-floor meeting areas at the same exhibition. Several meetings with major airport authority buyers were conducted in the privacy of the upper level — conversations that would not have been possible in a standard open-booth configuration.

This project demonstrated our ability to integrate heavy industrial demonstration equipment into a refined exhibition environment without compromising on either the technical requirements of the demo or the design quality of the stand. Vanderlande subsequently engaged us for their next regional exhibition appearance.
